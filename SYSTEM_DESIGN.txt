Scalability:

- ability of the system to cope with the increasing load.

What is Load?

- load can be anything for servers it could be requests/sec, for databases it could be data volume.


fan-out meaning - one service makes request to other services to process one incoming request.

Twitter Case Study: (Read to Write lopsidedness)

- Post Tweet: 12,000 req/sec, modern databases can handle it efficiently

- Home Timeline: This is all about getting the posts from all the people you follow (read intensive), 300k req/sec.


An imbalance was created between the read and write operation.

First Attempt: Joins were made between users, tweets and follower table to populate the relevant data. We know joins are computationally expensive and 300kreq/sec can 
just melt our databases. for quite a long time we sticked to this approach only.


Second Attempt: create separate cache for each follower meaning suppose a user u1 has followers f1, f2 and f3. f1, f2 and f3 will maintain their separate caches as soon
as user u1 posts something, all the followers of u1 are narrowed down and specific writes are made in the followers cache. So that whenever f1, f2 and f3 requires post,
it will be a constant O(1) look up in their caches. All heavy computations are excluded from the process.

Currently twitter is making use of both the approaches for the users with minimum followers twitter is going with 2nd attempt for individuals who posses a large follower
count they are being identified and first attempt is put into action.
